How AI shopping assistants are changing the way readers discover offers

Publisher demo page · Sponsored placement preview

Digital publishers are experimenting with new ad formats that feel more useful than static promotion. Instead of showing a single message, interactive placements can help readers compare products, explore travel ideas, or ask for timely recommendations.

The format on the right is a 300x600 rich media banner. It starts as a compact assistant inside the ad slot, then opens an AI chat experience when the reader taps a suggested question or types one of their own.

In a production publisher environment, this unit can remain constrained to the ad slot or use an approved expandable rich media container. The exact behavior depends on the ad server, SafeFrame settings, and publisher policy.

For this demo page, the banner is embedded inside a 300x600 iframe. When the assistant opens, the creative asks the parent page to expand that iframe over the viewport, which simulates a publisher-side expandable placement.

The result is a normal right-rail unit at rest, with the assistant modal appearing outside the original ad slot after the reader interacts with it.
